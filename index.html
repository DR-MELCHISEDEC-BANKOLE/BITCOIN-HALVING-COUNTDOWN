Bitcoin halving is a critical event in its blockchain, occurring approximately every four years (210,000 blocks).
Bitcoin halvings are associated with specific block heights, with the 2024 halving expected at block height 840,000.
The halving cycle occurs every 210,000 blocks, shaping the supply dynamics of Bitcoin.
The last halving was in May 2020, and the next is expected around April 2024, reducing the block reward from 6.25 to 3.125 bitcoins.

Previous halving event dates:

  • The first halving event: November 28, 2012 (UTC) at block height 210,000.
  • The second halving event: July 9, 2016 (UTC) at block height 420,000.
  • The third halving event: May 11, 2020 (UTC) at block height 630,000.

Bitcoin blockchain operates under the following consensus principles:

  • A fixed supply of 21 million Bitcoins is predetermined.
  • The network targets 10-minute intervals between blocks.
  • Halving events take place every 210,000 blocks, occurring roughly every four years.
  • The block reward, starting at 50, undergoes continual halving during each event until reaching zero, expected around the year 2140.
  • Any modification to these parameters necessitates unanimous agreement from all participants in the Bitcoin network.
